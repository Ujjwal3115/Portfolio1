Project Description

Event: Engineer's Day Idea Presentation Competition

Organized by: Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurugram

Project Title: BlazeBlocker - Automatic Fire Extinguishing System for Vehicles

Achievement: Secured 2nd Place

Description: Developed and presented an innovative concept for an automatic fire extinguishing system specifically designed for vehicles, earning 2nd place in the Engineer's Day Idea Presentation Competition. This system aims to enhance vehicle safety by proactively detecting and suppressing fires in critical areas. Our proposed solution integrates:

Multi-Sensor Technology: Utilizing a combination of fire and heat sensors strategically placed throughout the vehicle to ensure rapid and accurate fire detection.

Airbag Sensor Integration: Establishing a connection with the vehicle's airbag sensor system to trigger preemptive deployment of fire extinguishing substances upon impact, mitigating potential post-collision fire hazards.

Targeted Deployment: Employing a system to precisely deliver fire extinguishing agents to high-risk zones, including the engine bay and near the fuel tank, maximizing suppression efficiency and minimizing collateral damage.

This project, recognized with a 2nd place award, showcases our ability to identify real-world problems, conceptualize innovative engineering solutions, and integrate various sensor technologies for enhanced safety and automation in the automotive sector.
